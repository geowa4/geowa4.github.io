Life Automation

Technology is powerful. We are spending an increasing amount of time in front of computers, smartphones, and tablets. We check in to our favorite locations and tell the world what we're doing every minute of every day. All of the major tech firms have an immense amount of data about us, and we keep giving them more.

Every decision we make reduces our ability to make the next one; this concept is called decision fatigue. With all this data about how we live our lives, we should be able to reduce the amount of choices we have to make. Google Now is a good step, and I definitely use that to tell me everything from the weather to traffic to when my favorite teams are playing. I use IFTTT to automate the interactions between a lot of common services. For the lower level smartphone automation, I use Trigger. When it's time for me to leave for work, my automation services tell me how long it should take me to get there and the weather report for the day so I know what to wear. Upon arrival to work and home, my phone configures itself for the environment so I don't have to. And since I have a lot of meetings, having a trigger to create a note in Evernote when my next meeting starts is really useful.

Personally, I love watching this space mature. I think there's a lot of potential to make our lives easier. We should be putting as much in the world as possible so we can use our brains for bigger and better things.
